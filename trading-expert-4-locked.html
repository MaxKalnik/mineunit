What on Earth are trading pairs?

Dearest friends, the time has come to conclude our series of crypto trading courses. But let us not end on a sad note, but rather engage with more knowledge that can make your wallet beefier. Crypto Currency Pairs should do the trick just fine as they probably bring in the largest share of trading profits.

What is trading in pairs? It is a trade that does not require any involvement from fiat currencies. Let’s say you’ve traded Ether for Bitcoin and that would be a trading pair. It matters little which was the currency you bought or sold – the pair works both ways.

This opens an entirely new field of operations for traders as you can go 100% digital currency and make a huge profit from newly emerging coins.

Why bother?

Imagine you have both Bitcoin and cash. The cash is irrelevant and, just in case, I do not mean Bitcoin Cash. I am talking about the good old US dollars or Swiss Francs or whatever. What matters is that having both is fine and dandy by all standards… Until you wish to purchase, say, Litecoin.   

It’s not that you can’t buy Litecoin with cash. You can (although there are a lot of strictly digital trade currencies). That is not the point. The point is the volatility of the cryptocurrencies. Let’s assume that your Bitcoin, all of a sudden, jumps in price by 5%, 10%, or even 20%. With that kind of money, you can purchase 5%, 10% and 20% more of Litecoin accordingly while the cash you own stays at the same point.

Same goes both ways and truly shines in the buy low sell high paradigm.

Still doesn’t make sense to you? Who cares if you have 10% more of Litecoin if the equivalent to US dollars is the same? Well, you should as you firmly believe that Litecoin will go up in price in the near future and you will be able to sell it for more cash.

How can you know that? Because you are a smart trader and you have performed both types of analysis discussed in the earlier courses.
