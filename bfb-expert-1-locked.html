Your Ultimate Guide to a Blockchain Startup-Unicorn

  • Course by: Alex Seryj
  • Expert
  • Released: May 25, 2018
2 Likes

ICO looks like the new cool kid in the crowdfunding block/ But why did it gain enough traction to make almost a quarter of all earning in Bitcoin? Is it a good fit for your business and, if so, how to launch one successfully? The answers are at your fingertips.

Blockchain is more than Bitcoin!

Bitcoin and other Altcoins are everywhere nowadays. The news and major media outlets publish new stories on a daily basis, new shops, companies and even governments choose to accept them as a valid currency and so on and so forth.

The price of the coin heats up the interest of the masses and creates new opportunities for entire communities of miners, traders and investors.

The media generates more news, the community grows even further, they pitch in and bring something new, the media writes about it and the cycle loops. No wonder that most of us only hear about Altcoin as it is a self-autonomous ecosystem compiled of viral content.

But the media can’t be the only reason for the popularity of Bitcoin and Altcoin. There was, after all, a time when no one new nor wrote about it at Forbes, the Huffington Post or Mashable. And yet it still grew high enough to attract that kind of attention.

What was it? What’s really juicing up the price of Bitcoin? The Blockchain network, of course. It is the real unsung hero.

The power of the Blockchain

But what makes the Blockchain network big enough for supporting coins to be worth several hundreds or even thousands of dollars?

Here is an example.

You have an iTunes account on your iPhone, don’t you? It is a digital asset, as you own something of value that is not real but composed entirely of bits and bytes. Well, can you imagine that before the introduction of Blockchain there was no way for validating your ownership?

Digital relationships were built entirely on trust. You trusted Apple to let you use your content on their servers after logging in with a valid username/password string. You simply had no choice but to trust your data into the hands of a corporation like Google, Apple, Facebook or Microsoft and, while those big guys have a reputation and seem like trustworthy enough, same cannot be said about other digital asset providers.

Just think about the amount of scams and phishing websites all over the internet that can take your money or time and give absolutely nothing in return.

This can never happen in the Blockchain network. No, not because it is more trustworthy than any other digital systems, but because it is the opposite of it. The Blockchain network is trustless (remember our talk about the smart contracts from the previous courses?). It simply has no need for such a vague concept.

Ok, digital assets aside, let’s talk ownership. Of what? Of anything and everything. Let’s imagine you have the stock of a company and you wish to sell it. What do you have to do today? Start with hiring a lawyer, prepare yourself for stacks of paperwork and brace the need in a centralized regulator or a third-party authority such as the NY Stock Exchange as those guys have the trust of the community.
