Mining Hardware, the difference between CPU, GPU and ASIC mining (LEVEL – Savvy)

  • Course by: Alex Seryj
  • Savvy
  • Released: May 25, 2018
2 Likes

What on Earth is an ASIC chip? Why are GPU cards used for mining over CPUs? How to set up an efficient rig and earn instead of wasting investments? We will provide the answers to these and other questions over the most compelling course on crypto mining up-to-date

Why are you here?

$12.8 Million. This is the latest mining reward for the past 24 hours (at least at the moment we wrote this course). Hold on for a moment. Let that thought sink in.

Miners have earned nearly thirteen million dollars during the course of one day and one night in Bitcoin alone. There are 1.600+ other currencies out there. If this is not a good enough reason to get a slice of the mining pie for yourself, what is?

Not only that but you probably have a friend who has a mate who has a sister, and that sister’s uncle has made a fortune mining. This is not exactly what one might call “from the first hands” info, right? But it is enough to light a spark. “I can do the same”, you think. “But can I?”

Can you?

Technically, you can. Without a doubt.  Alas, as it always is with money, free cheese can only be found in a mousetrap. Mining is pretty much a day-to-day business that requires skill, dedication, and a fair share of investment.

The “I want to make money out of air” mindset doesn’t work and it will not deliver any results without help. You have Mineunit, our technological know-how and industry expertise on your side for that. But if you wish to mine on your own, please put on your entrepreneur hat. You will need it. A lot.

But where does your journey start? At an in-depth look at what mining really is, of course!

Crypto mining in a nutshell

Mining stands on two whales. The first one is the process of adding transactions into the blockchain network and the other is the creation of new currencies and finding the nonse to generate these blocks. To do that a miner needs sophisticated hardware and software components that form a mining rig.
